My Entrepreneural Journey

From Observation to Action

My journey began with a troubling realization: while the tech industry desperately needs talent, countless brilliant young people remain locked out simply because they can't afford the training required to break in. I witnessed youth in my community—creative, ambitious, hungry to learn—watching opportunities pass them by because existing training programs cost thousands of dollars they didn't have.

The contradiction was staggering. Companies complained about talent shortages while young people sat idle, unable to access the very education that could fill those gaps. Traditional education systems weren't adapting fast enough, and private bootcamps catered exclusively to those who could afford premium prices. Someone needed to build the bridge. That's when I knew this wasn't just an observation—it was a calling.

The Problem: Youth Trapped Between Ambition and Access

Youth unemployment isn't just a statistic; it's a crisis of wasted potential. Globally, young people are three times more likely to be unemployed than adults, with rates in some regions exceeding 25%. The technology sector, despite its explosive growth, remains largely inaccessible to youth from financially constrained backgrounds.

The Core Barriers:

  • Financial Exclusions:
    • Quality tech training programs cost between $10,000 and $20,000, placing them completely out of reach for most young people. Scholarships are limited and highly competitive. Student loans require credit history many youth don't have. The result? Talent goes undiscovered and undeveloped.
  • The Experience Paradox:
    • Entry-level jobs demand 2-3 years of experience—an impossible requirement that traps youth in a cycle where they can't get hired without experience and can't gain experience without being hired. Even when they acquire skills independently, they lack the credentials and portfolio work that employers trust.
  • Information Asymmetry:
    • Young people in underserved communities often don't know which tech skills are in demand, how to access training, or how to position themselves for employment. They lack mentors who understand the industry's unwritten rules and networks that make opportunities visible.
  • Skills Market Mismatch:
    • Even when youth pursue education, many programs teach outdated technologies or fail to emphasize the practical, project-based learning that employers value. Graduates emerge with certificates but without the hands-on experience to thrive in real work environments.
  • Validated Data:
    • The International Labour Organization reports that 73 million young people globally are seeking work
    • Studies show that youth from low-income backgrounds are 40% less likely to receive any form of professional training
    • The cybersecurity industry alone faces a shortage of 3.4 million professionals, while web development positions grow by 13% annually
    • Employers report that 60% of entry-level candidates lack the practical skills needed despite having formal education

    This isn't a problem of motivation or capability. It's a problem of access, affordability, and alignment.

Our Solution

Affordable Tech Training That Opens Doors

We're building a venture that demolishes financial barriers to tech education by providing high-quality, job-ready training in Web Development and Cybersecurity at prices youth can actually afford. But we go far beyond just teaching code—we create complete pathways from learning to employment.

How Our Solution Works

  • Accessible Pricing Model:
    • We offer training at a fraction of traditional bootcamp costs through a tiered pricing system that includes full scholarships for the most financially vulnerable, pay-what-you-can options, and income-share agreements where students pay only after they're employed. No one is turned away because of financial constraints.
  • Industry-Aligned Curriculum:
    • Our programs are designed in direct consultation with hiring companies to ensure every skill taught is immediately applicable. In Web Development, students master HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, and deployment—the exact stack employers seek. In Cybersecurity, they learn network security, ethical hacking, threat analysis, and compliance frameworks that organizations desperately need.
  • Project-Based Learning:
    • Students don't just watch videos; they build real applications, solve actual security challenges, and create portfolios that demonstrate capability to employers. Every graduate completes at least three substantial projects they can showcase in interviews.
  • Mentorship and Career Support:
    • We connect each student with industry mentors who provide guidance, review their work, and make introductions. We offer resume building, interview preparation, and direct connections to partner companies actively hiring.
  • Flexible Learning Paths:
    • Recognizing that many youth must work while learning, we offer both intensive full-time tracks (12 weeks) and part-time evening/weekend options (24 weeks) that accommodate existing responsibilities.
  • Community and Belonging:
    • We build cohorts where students learn together, support each other, and form networks that extend beyond graduation. Isolation is replaced with community; doubt is replaced with confidence.

Implementation Plan:

  • Phase 1 (Months 1-3):
    • Launch pilot program with 30 students in Web Development. Establish partnerships with three local tech companies for internship placement. Secure initial funding through grants and impact investors.
  • Phase 2 (Months 4-6):
    • Introduce Cybersecurity track. Scale to 50 students per cohort. Hire two additional instructors with industry experience. Launch job placement platform connecting graduates with employers.
  • Phase 3 (Months 7-12):
    • Expand to second location. Develop corporate partnership program where companies sponsor students in exchange for recruitment pipeline. Achieve 70% job placement rate within three months of graduation.
  • Phase 4 (Year 2+):
    • Scale to multiple cities. Introduce advanced specialization tracks. Build alumni network that creates peer-to-peer mentorship and job referrals.
